# Subject: Concept Proposal for the One Mandaluyong Mobile Application – A Unified Digital Citizen Services Platform for the City of Mandaluyong

The introduction can briefly acknowledge the City's efforts toward digital transformation and explain that your proposal is an independent concept intended to support—not replace—existing government initiatives.

Then introduce the vision:

The One Mandaluyong App is envisioned as a unified mobile platform that serves as the primary digital gateway between the City Government of Mandaluyong and its residents. Rather than requiring citizens to navigate multiple offices, websites, or manual processes, the application aims to consolidate local government services into a single, secure, AI-assisted, and citizen-centered experience.

AI-Assisted Features
Instead of a simple chatbot, propose an AI assistant that can:

answer questions about city services,

guide citizens through application processes,

recommend the correct office,

estimate processing requirements,

summarize application status,

explain city ordinances in plain language,

detect incomplete submissions before filing,

provide multilingual assistance.

City Operations
You can also suggest capabilities for city employees:

service request dashboards,

analytics,

processing queues,

performance monitoring,

citizen satisfaction metrics,

document tracking,

workflow automation,

cross-department coordination.

Engineering and Governance
This is where your previous research becomes useful without mentioning AIEIP, PHILOS, or CIVIS.

Suggest that the platform should be built with:

AI-assisted decision support

repository-first documentation

institutional knowledge management

reusable software architecture

capability-oriented design

interoperability with existing national platforms where appropriate

cybersecurity and privacy by design

continuous improvement through citizen feedback and operational metrics

Then end with something like:

My intention is not to replace existing systems or duplicate national initiatives, but to present an independent concept that could strengthen digital service delivery at the local government level. If the City Government believes these ideas may be valuable, I would be honored to further discuss or develop the concept in collaboration with the appropriate offices.

I actually think this proposal has a higher chance of generating interest than a national-scale proposal because it addresses a specific local government with a concrete, achievable vision. If developed well, One Mandaluyong could even serve as a pilot or model for other LGUs while remaining aligned with the Philippines' broader e-governance initiatives.
